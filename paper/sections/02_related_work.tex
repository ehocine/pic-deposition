\section{Related Work}
Early PIC studies established that spatial grid resolution and shape-function choice control aliasing noise and numerical heating~\cite{langdon1970,okuda1972}.
Brackbill and Barnes showed that discrete charge non-conservation can destabilize long-time integrations~\cite{brackbill1980}, motivating exact deposition schemes.
Esirkepov's trajectory-based method provides rigorous charge conservation for arbitrary shape factors~\cite{esirkepov2001}, while Eastwood's virtual-particle formulation extended conservative deposition to electromagnetic PIC~\cite{eastwood1981}.

PIConGPU~\cite{burau2010,huebl2019} targets large-scale electromagnetic PIC on heterogeneous supercomputers, combining Esirkepov or EZ current deposition~\cite{steiniger2023} with supercell-local caching on GPUs.
Smilei~\cite{derouillat2018} couples cycle-sort particle reordering with SIMD-vectorized deposition; Beck et al.\ report operator speedups of up to $\sim$3$\times$ when sorting enables vectorized projection~\cite{beck2019}.
Harvey et al.\ implemented charge-conserving deposition on hybrid CPU--GPU systems with up to $\sim$4$\times$ GPU speedup over CPU baselines on tested nodes~\cite{harvey2015}.
Viereck et al., in a CPC survey, concluded that PIC scatter is memory-bandwidth limited and that privatized or tiled accumulation can outperform naive atomics when contention is high~\cite{viereck2016}.
Wilke et al.\ and Huebl et al.\ discussed GPU PIC strategies and cluster scalability~\cite{wilke2017,huebl2014}.

Our contribution is a lightweight 2D electrostatic reference that separates sort from deposit-kernel timing and couples microbenchmarks with charge, energy, spectral-noise, Langmuir-wave, two-stream, and long-run conservation validation under a common runtime interface.
